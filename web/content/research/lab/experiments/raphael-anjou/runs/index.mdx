Every page here is one search run with a name, an idea, and a board. They share
the [engines](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/engines); what differs is
how each one steers them: what it seeds, what it forbids, what it tears up and
rebuilds.

Most of these runs are pipelines rather than single algorithms: a run builds a
board with one engine, then lifts or finishes it with another. Each page's method
section walks its stages in order. Several of the from-scratch builders lean on
the beam producer, and two on the ALNS repair loop, engines that do not yet have
their own write-ups here (see the [engines page](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/engines));
where a run does, its page says so plainly rather than pointing at a page that is
not up yet.

They are ordered by what they taught, not by score. A run that ended lower but
explained why is worth more here than one that scraped a point and could not say
how.
