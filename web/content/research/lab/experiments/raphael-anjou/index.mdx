These are [Raphaël Anjou's](/research/people/raphael-anjou) search experiments.
Some are original ideas; some faithfully reimplement a known community
technique to measure exactly what it buys. Each write-up has the idea, the
board it reached, and the questions it left open. The best reaches 463 of 480
matched edges; the community's best on the same puzzle is 470. Methods and
boards are here in full.

Most of these experiments are not built from nothing. They run on a small set
of shared [engines](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/engines): a
constructive beam producer, a destroy-and-repair local search, and a family of
CSP presets, documented once and reused across study after study. If you want
to know how a search actually works before reading what a particular study did
with it, start there.

Two studies stand apart from the named runs below, one for each way people
attack this puzzle. The [DFS study](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/dfs-study)
takes the depth-first backtracker apart, building a whole family of them one
change at a time (fill order, heuristic, break policy). Its sibling, the
[repair study](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/repair-study), does the
same for destroy-and-repair local search (which region to destroy, how to rebuild
it, when to keep a move, what board to start from), the loop the records actually
reach the top with. Both measure one change at a time on the same ten variants,
and both are measurements rather than single scored solvers, so each has its own
leaderboard rather than a row on the chart below.

A third grouping cuts across the runs by what they have in common rather than by
paradigm: the experiments that
[learn from strong boards](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/learning). Five
of the runs below (PRIOR, KEYRING, LODESTONE, PALIMPSEST and REPLAY) do not reason
only from the puzzle's rules; they mine the corpus of boards already found for
structure and feed it back into a search. Their method-agnostic write-up is the
[learning-from-boards theory section](/research/build/learning), and the hub gathers
what each one learned and where all five stop.
