These are [Raphaël Anjou's](/research/people/raphael-anjou) search experiments.
Some are original ideas; some faithfully reimplement a known community
technique to measure exactly what it buys. Each write-up has the idea, the
board it reached, and the questions it left open. The best reaches 463 of 480
matched edges; the community's best on the same puzzle is 470. Methods and
boards are here in full.

Most of these experiments are not built from nothing. They run on a small set
of shared [engines](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/engines): a
constructive beam producer, a destroy-and-repair local search, and a family of
CSP presets, documented once and reused across study after study. If you want
to know how a search actually works before reading what a particular study did
with it, start there.

Three studies stand apart from the combination pipelines below, each holding one
question steady and varying one thing about it. The
[DFS study](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/dfs-study) takes the
depth-first backtracker apart, building a whole family of them one change at a
time (fill order, heuristic, break policy). The
[repair study](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/repair-study) does the same
for destroy-and-repair local search (which region to destroy, how to rebuild it,
when to keep a move, what board to start from), the loop the records actually reach
the top with. The third,
[learning from strong boards](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/learning),
turns the question inward: rather than varying the search, it varies what the
search is allowed to *know*, mining the corpus of boards already found for
structure (PRIOR, KEYRING, LODESTONE, PALIMPSEST, REPLAY) and feeding it back in.
Its method-agnostic write-up is the
[learning-from-boards theory section](/research/build/learning). All three measure
one thing at a time and each has its own home. The DFS and repair studies vary a
knob across a grid rather than producing one board, so they carry their own
leaderboards instead of a row on the chart below; the five learning experiments
each reach a single scored board, so they keep their rows here (labelled by score)
as well as their study home.
