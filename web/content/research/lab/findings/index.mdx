The standing structural results about the puzzle, each written up with the
computation behind it. They fall into three kinds: signatures of the design,
walls that no local method gets past, and proofs of why the records are
stuck. Every one is reproducible, and each entry below links to its full
write-up under [Why it's hard](/research/why), with the exact computation and
a reproduce command.

## Design signatures

- [Designed to be unsolvable: the recipe](/research/why/design-recipe): the
  full ingredient list for the hardest possible edge-matching puzzle, which
  the rest of this section documents one signature at a time.
- [Tuned to the hardness peak](/research/why/phase-transition): 17 interior
  colors and 5 frame colors put the puzzle on the difficulty peak by design.
- [The rare colors live on the frame](/research/why/rare-color-geography):
  five colors appear only on the border ring, each on exactly 24 edges,
  balanced on purpose.
- [The border balance (NS-1)](/research/why/border-balance): the border
  hands the interior exactly the colors it hands back, a real necessary
  condition with a revealing blind spot.

## Structural walls

- [Forbidden patterns](/research/why/forbidden-patterns): 99.72% of 2×2
  placements are impossible; a real solution contains none.
- [No forced moves](/research/why/no-forced-moves): yet no piece is ever
  pinned, since every interior piece has 73 to 137 neighbours.
- [Entropy and the area law](/research/why/entropy-area-law): the matching
  grammar is rich; the use-each-piece-once rule collapses it past ~80 cells.

## Rigidity proofs

- [The rigidity wall](/research/why/rigidity-wall): integer programming
  proves every record board is locally frozen, out to radius 4.
- [Why basin-hopping is impossible](/research/why/sigma-cycles): two great
  boards differ by one 154-cell cycle; every smaller piece of it scores worse.
- [Where the mismatches live](/research/why/mismatch-geometry): a record
  board packs all its errors into one five-row band; scan direction decides
  which end.
- [Where you place the hints beats how many](/research/why/hint-geometry): on
  an E2-like 16×16, eighteen scattered hints beat eighty contiguous ones,
  because nearly all the search cost lives in the endgame.

To see how each wall maps to the methods that hit it, read
