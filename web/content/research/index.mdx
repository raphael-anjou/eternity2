This wiki is for the people who want to actually solve it. Everyone who has
attacked Eternity II took one of nine roads, from modelling why it resists,
through pruning the search and building boards up, to throwing hardware at the wall.
Pick the road you care about; each opens onto everything the wiki knows about
it, drawn from the theory, the solver-building guides, and the lab notebook
alike.

<RootsDiagram />

Prefer to browse by reading mode rather than by method? The wiki splits four
ways:

<div className="not-prose grid gap-4 md:grid-cols-2">
  <Door to="/research/why" title="Why it's hard">
    The design that was tuned to resist cleverness, and the structural walls
    (rigidity, entropy, forbidden patterns) that explain the gap between the
    best known board and a full solution.
  </Door>
  <Door to="/research/build" title="Build a solver">
    Validation data, the literature ranked by usefulness, the methods behind
    the records, the dead ends, and how to run the code yourself.
  </Door>
  <Door to="/research/lab" title="The lab">
    The open notebook: structural findings and the named search experiments
    built to attack the puzzle, each credited to its author and reproducible
    from source.
  </Door>
  <Door to="/research/community" title="History & community">
    The record and the people behind it: two decades told as a story, the
    record boards themselves, the record-holders and theorists, and the papers.
  </Door>
</div>

Or browse by method instead: the nine roads in the left rail are each a hub
gathering every article on that theme.

## The reference shelf

Two lookups you will reach for again and again while reading or building:

<div className="not-prose grid gap-3 md:grid-cols-2">

    The exact counts (pieces, colours, edges) to check your own edge-matching

  </Door>
  <Door to="/research/glossary" title="Glossary">
    Every term the wiki leans on, defined once: community jargon, the loaded
    computer-science words, and the notation the boards are written in.
  </Door>

The story of the puzzle, its records and the people who chased them now live
together under [History & community](/research/community).
