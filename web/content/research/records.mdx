## Notebook lineage: how the project's own numbers were reached

This section is about the project's notebook, not the community record ladder.
Nothing here is a community record: every mark in the tables above (470 in the
starter-only regime, 464 with all five clues) sits above every row below. The
rows exist so the project's own numbers can be traced, each with its scoring
convention spelled out. The methods behind them are documented in
[the lab](/research/lab).

| Date | Board | Score | Scoring convention | How |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 2026-07-13 | Producer board, raw | 457/480 | All five clues at their official cells; matched edges out of 480 | A beam producer running a comb-shaped fill order; this is the raw board, before any repair. |
| 2026-07-13 | Same board, after repair | 461/480 | All five clues at their official cells; matched edges out of 480 | A destroy-and-repair pass lifted the raw 457 through 459 and 460 to 461. The loop itself is studied in [the repair study](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/repair-study). |
| 2026-07 | PALIMPSEST | 463/480 | Starter clue only; matched edges out of 480 | A placement prior mined from the community board corpus, plus targeted destroy-and-repair. Written up in [PALIMPSEST](/research/lab/experiments/raphael-anjou/learning/palimpsest). |

The two conventions do not compare: a starter-only score and a five-clue score
aim at different targets, which is why each row names its own. Under the
five-clue convention the project's best is 461 against the community's 464;
under starter-only its best is 463 against the community's 470.
