# Digest 0007 — 2009-03 → 2009-08
Coverage: 394 messages, msg_num 6567–6961.

Post-scrutiny plateau: no new official scores, but the window closes three
running stories — the Hungarian ELTE-team claim collapses, Louis Verhaard's
public solver is independently benchmarked to a 467, and Verhaard himself
returns in August with the definitive first-person account of the winning
entry ("Anna is my wife"). Meanwhile the group's centre of gravity shifts
from record-chasing to exact-counting collaborations (3x3/4x4 partial
censuses cross-verified by half a dozen independent programs), continuous/
LP relaxation experiments, and a wave of open-sourced tooling. The comb
search writeup Verhaard promised "after new year" never materialises: he
states in March he has done no further work on the puzzle.

Numbering caveat: msg 6879's subject celebrates "message 7000" — original
Yahoo message numbers ran ~120 ahead of the groups.io msg_num used here.
Same for in-thread references (e.g. "posting 6407/6406" in msg 6682).

### 2009-07-31 — Al Hopfer's "creation of E2" theory: colour-in generation and the balance argument
- who: Al Hopfer; andreas gammel; nc9201

### 2009-08-11 — Implicit solvers: continuous relaxation stalls at 420–440 edges
- who: bozmo2004 (Andrew); okifinoki (Benjamin); leos251 (Leo); nc9201
- what: Andrew (bozmo2004) describes an "implicit solver": a 256×256×4 weight grid (~250k numbers) where "every piece is everywhere", scored by a smooth energy function and iterated by Newton-Raphson toward a maximum — with the geometry problem that the unit hypercube's diagonal is 500 units long, plus instabilities where sub-areas solve locally. ### 2009-08-27 — Artificial hints: non-touching piece sets as calculated guesses
- who: mrjohngilbert; nc9201; Al Hopfer; Eternity Blogger
- what: For possibility-matrix solvers starved of hints, John Gilbert proposes "artificial hints": sets of inner pieces that share no patterns and therefore can never touch — sets as large as six exist, e.g. (83, 102, 160, 173, 199, 207).
