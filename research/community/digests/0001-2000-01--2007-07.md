### 2007-02-03 — Brendan Owen's solver, search-time curves, and the "24 borders" guess
- who: Brendan Owen, guenter stertenbrink, Alan O'Donnell

### 2007-06-23 — The planted-border anomaly: paired edges inflate border solutions
- who: Brendan Owen, guenter stertenbrink, angwin_uk, Dave Clark
- what: Owen found empirically that piece sets with a planted solution have a far higher branching factor on the border puzzle than the naive theory or random sets predict, and isolated the cause: simply ensuring every edge colour occurs in pairs reproduces the effect (msg 422). Eddy and Stertenbrink developed the counting side (clockwise/counterclockwise colour-count matching; probability ~4.66e-12 that a random 16-colour set matches all counts, msg 485/496; ~2^B correction factor, msg 412). This is the earliest statement of the border/count-balance effect later central to solution-count estimates.

### 2007-07-30 — First score talk and the high-score reality check
- who: Dave Clark, Brendan Owen, ad2402, matt burns
- what: Two days after launch, members reported partial scores: eternity2.net's free solver reached >450/480 "within a few minutes" — but by placing non-matching pieces to maximise score, not by tiling correctly (Clark, msg 1177/1181). ad2402 claimed "scores over 400" and a target of 450+ before submitting (msg 1157). ### 2007-07-31 — New arrivals at launch: Sergio Demian Lerner and the "phantom sets" warning
- who: Sergio Demian Lerner (slerner), madcowjens, Craig Easton
