### 2008-03-04 — Brendan's "strip back" experiment: last 149 pieces are nearly forced

### 2008-04-23 — Design-space experiments: why 5/17 and why scanline
- who: Brendan Owen
- what: Brendan tests whether E2's 5 border/17 middle split really is the hardest design by generating comparable 16x16 puzzles at 2/19 and 14/15 splits and analysing scanline, middle-first, and border-first search orders on each. ### 2008-06-25 — antminder: 462 per day from a backtracker+GA hybrid; the Schaus VLNS neighborhood
- who: antminder; pietboss (Pierre Schaus); guenter stertenbrink; Anurag Sahay
- what: antminder reports his latest high-score program averages a little over one day per 462/480 (best in 8 days of running): a backtracker places pieces for ~3 minutes leaving a rectangular hole, then a steady-state GA with a repair operator maximizes partial score, then restart. The repair operator comes from the paper Pierre Schaus posted to the files section ("papers_jfpc"): remove a set of non-adjacent pieces and re-place them *optimally* by solving an assignment problem (Hungarian/Munkres, O(n^3)); Schaus explains up to half the board (chessboard pattern) can be removed and optimally refilled, though they found moving >16 pieces not useful. antminder is explicit that this targets partial score, not solving — the GA "makes it worse" for finding a full solution. This is the archive's first detailed account of the large-neighborhood partial-score technique behind the coming record climbs.
- source: msg_num 5589 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/5589 — "stochastic algorithms and high partial scores" (Schaus on Munkres 5601; worse-for-solving 5591)
- wiki-relevance: records, technique:partial-score, technique:vlns
- note: pietboss is Pierre Schaus, who posted the first score claim (410) in Aug 2007 (msg 1568).
