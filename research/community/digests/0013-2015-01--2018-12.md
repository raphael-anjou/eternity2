# Digest 0013 — 2015-01 → 2018-12
Coverage: 461 messages, msg_num 9360–9833 (151 in 2015, 143 in 2016, 125 in 2017, 42 in 2018).

Window context: four more prize-less years, but the most consequential window
since the contest itself — because in 2017 Brendan Owen's 10x10 benchmark
FALLS. Peter McGavin solves set_1 10x10 in September 2017 by industrialising
exactly the method the community had converged on (enumerate first rows, rank
them by complex theory, farm them to hundreds of cores), and the result lands
within the theory's error bars — the strongest empirical validation Brendan's
complex theory ever received. Two months later McGavin places all 256 real E2
pieces on a 16x16 board with all 480 internal joins matched ("unframed E2"),
the closest anyone has come to a physical E2-like solution. The same window
quietly detonates a records bombshell: Dima surfaces Naohiro Takahashi's
(chokudai's) 2009 claim of 468/480 — never verified, left unresolved. Around
these peaks: a cheap-hardware efficiency race (Raspberry Pi, Parallella,
ODROID, nodes-per-second-per-watt tables), David Barr's GPU/OpenCL and cloud
experiments, the arrival of Adam Miles with an Xbox One X compute-shader
solver that exhaustively re-verifies the 9x9 census, and a second Wauters-group
academic paper. Meanwhile the Czech 12,000 EUR re-prize (deadline 2015-07-31)
expires without a single mention on the list, and Yahoo Groups itself decays —
2018 produces just 42 messages.

## Notable events (chronological)

### 2015-04-03 — David Barr's OpenCL solver works; code goes on GitHub
- who: David Barr; Peter McGavin; Arnaud Carré
- what: Barr gets his OpenCL solver running: row 1407888 of Martin's 10x10 first-row list (the row containing the known solution) is fully searched in 3 h 46 m on an AMD Radeon HD 7870 GPU, with the work divided 1620 ways (9364). His CPU baseline is 120M placements/s using all 8 cores of an FX-8120 (9366). He publishes the source at github.com/david3x3x3/eternity2 (9367) — one of the few open-source E2 solvers of the era, reused throughout the window.

### 2015-05-23 — Verhaard declares Brendan's estimated_time.pdf outdated; McGavin posts the modern numbers
- who: louis.verhaard; Peter McGavin; Dan Karlsson; guenter stertenbrink
- what: Asked about the old 10^25-years folklore, Louis Verhaard states the estimates in Brendan's estimated_time.pdf were based on "simple theory" and are outdated (9385); he can no longer find his own 463→470 time estimates and doubts their use for predicting a 480 (9389). Verhaard: "I have not seen such thorough analysis before. Great work!" (9394).

### 2015-06-02 — "Réduction superpolynomiale": fixed-orientation E2 is easy, and Schaus resurfaces
- who: (moderated newcomer, via Ole Knudsen); Michael Field; pierre.schaus; juraj.pivovarov; Guillaume L.; void solid
- what: A French newcomer's claim of a superpolynomial complexity reduction (solve orientations first, then permutation) gets the classic treatment. Pierre Schaus points to his 2008 JFPC paper with a polynomial-time algorithm for the permutation phase (9400); Juraj calls it one of the best-written E2 papers, then implements the idea in 55 minutes of Python and solves 100 fixed-orientation 16x16 instances in 108 s total (9401/9404), demolishing the mystique: the hard part is finding THE correct orientation of all 256 tiles (9411). void solid's 32x32 "mega puzzle without rotations" reduction is dismantled by Ole and dvholten (9413/9414/9418). Guillaume L. concedes and publishes a "Theseus" essay instead (9407/9420).
- source: msg_num 9400 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/9400 — "Réduction superpolynomiale de la complexité du puzzle Eternity II" (Juraj's demo 9404/9406, orientation-is-the-problem 9411, mega-puzzle rebuttal 9414)

### 2017-09-18 — BRENDAN'S 10x10 SOLVED: McGavin finds the first new set_1 solution
- who: Peter McGavin; David Barr; mrjohngilbert; capiman26061973 (Martin)
- what: McGavin posts the first discovered new solution to Brendan's set_1 10x10 — different from the seed solution the puzzle was built from (9686). Method (9688): enumerate the ~20M first-row permutations; rank rows by complex-theory chance-of-solution per search-tree node; run backtrackers on every core available — ~20 at home, grown with 3 ODROID XU4s and 25 $12 Orange Pi Lites to 130+ cores, plus borrowed work servers for 400+ total. The solution arrived on test ~92,907 of a predicted ~1-per-70,000 — after ~2e+17 nodes (~180 core-years) and under 0.5% of the whole tree, with a full max-depth histogram published. "No new methods, just systematic persistence and the law of large numbers." Martin independently validates the solution (9725). John Gilbert: "Amazing that it is being truly just as hard as predicted" (9693) — the benchmark that digest 0012 called the communal battlefield is won, and complex theory is vindicated in the same stroke.
- source: msg_num 9686 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/9686 — "Brendan's set_1 10x10 solution" (method + stats 9688, validation 9725, as-hard-as-predicted 9693, what-next 9692)
- wiki-relevance: records, technique:benchmarks, technique:complex-theory

### 2017-10-11 — Half-board meet-in-the-middle debated and dismissed by the numbers
- who: dvholten; Peter McGavin; Dima; Dan Karlsson
- what: In the 10x10 thread's afterglow, dvholten revives the half-puzzle idea (generate many north halves, match against south halves). McGavin's counter is complex theory at its bluntest: ~6.5e+45 half-board "proposals" of which only ~14,000 lead to solutions, so random pairing is hopeless (9716/9717). The exchange ends with McGavin's much-quoted forecast: if E2 is ever solved, it will likely be by "an expert in combinatorics who figures out a straightforward formula for whether or not an area can be filled by a given set of pieces" (9722). Scanline-order orthodoxy is restated by Dan Karlsson with Brendan's theory as the measuring stick (9706/9709).

### 2017-11-12 — "Unframed E2" solved: all 480 internal joins matched with the real 256 pieces
- who: Peter McGavin; juraj.pivovarov; Henk van der Griendt; Dima; David Barr; Benoit
- what: McGavin sets himself a variant: place all 256 E2 pieces on the 16x16 board treating gray edges as ordinary colours (border pieces end up in an interior band), maximizing internal joins. On Nov 2 he posts 479/480 (9736); on Nov 12 a backtracker finds, from his ~4,400th 14x15 sub-solution, not one but thousands of full 480/480 configurations (9750, first grid posted; picture 9755). He estimates the difficulty on par with an unframed 14x14 from the 196 middle pieces (9719) and later analyzes which border-band designs are even possible (9781/9783); Barr and Dima contribute alternative legal layouts (9784/9785).
- source: msg_num 9750 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/9750 — "Unframed Eternity II" (479 first 9736, picture 9755, physical check 9754, not-E2 clarification 9757/9766, design space 9781–9786)
