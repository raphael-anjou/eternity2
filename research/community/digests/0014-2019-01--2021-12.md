# Digest 0014 — 2019-01 → 2021-12
Coverage: 647 messages, msg_num 9834–10584 plus msg_num 1–7 (see note).

Archive-numbering note: Yahoo Groups shut down mid-window. Messages posted
natively on the brand-new eternity2@groups.io in Oct–Nov 2019 got msg_num 1–7;
the Yahoo archive was then imported (preserving the old sequence up to ~9933)
and subsequent posts continue from there. A few msg_nums in the window are
absent from the export (deleted/moderated messages).

Window context: the record wave. The window opens as a low-traffic hobbyist
list (147 msgs in 2019, 103 in 2020), survives the death of Yahoo Groups, and
then produces the most consequential eighteen months since 2008: Joshua
Blackwood — a complete outsider who first surfaced via Reddit — posts a 468
(Aug 2020), open-sources his solver (Sep 2020), Peter McGavin runs it on ~200
cores and lands the 469 that is still the community ceiling (Sep 2020), Jef
Bucas ports it to C and a wave of further 469s follows (Nov 2020), and
Blackwood himself closes the arc with a 470 (Mar 2021) before retiring from
the puzzle. Around the records, the era's infrastructure is built: Bucas's
e2.bucas.name viewer (launched Nov 2019) becomes the community's shared board
format, and 2021's newcomer influx (397 msgs, the busiest year since 2010)
brings both an energetic solver-benchmark culture and the group's first-ever
moderation policy.
