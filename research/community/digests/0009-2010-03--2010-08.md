(2010-12-31). Traffic is thin (a whole month, April, has ~5 messages) and the

### 2010-03-01 — Frames from 7x1 metatiles: one frame, a billion arrangements
- who: Michael Field; Ole Knudsen; mrjohngilbert; louis.verhaard
- what: Michael Field builds complete frames from the 4 corners plus eight 7x1 border metatiles (managed as bitmaps) and counts how many ways each metatile's pieces can be internally rearranged while meeting the end constraints. ### 2010-08-13 — Balanced-colour bipartitions: easy to find, uncorrelated with the real solution
- who: leenstrab (Bruce); juraj.pivovarov (Juraj, M.Sc.)
- what: Bruce observes that on a checkerboard colouring of a solved board, exactly half of each colour's edges land on white squares, and proposes enumerating colour-balanced tile bipartitions to halve the search. Juraj runs the experiment on a solved sample puzzle: chromatic bipartitions are very easy to find (encoding colour counts as base-50 integers, then set partitioning), but their overlap with the solution's true checkerboard bipartition averages ~64/128 — exactly chance level, across 20 random bipartitions (7942). A clean, quantified negative result; Juraj leaves open the orientation-balance question (per-colour top/bottom and left/right sums equal, 7939) and Bruce keeps hunting a Gray-code-style enumeration (7943).
