# Digest 0015 — 2022-01 → 2026-03
Coverage: 1,068 messages, msg_num 10585–11823.

Window context: the modern era, ending at the archive's edge (last export message
2026-03-31). Message volume is steady (~210–260/yr) and the community has settled
into four durable activities: (1) the Blackwood-solver ecosystem — Jef Bucas's
e2.bucas.name viewer and libblackwood C port make 467 a *calibration target*
("about 30 × 467" in a week, msg 11001) while the canonical ceiling stays where
window 0014 left it (469 canonical / 470 one-clue, restated by Juraj at 11343 and
tied by Jef himself at 11401); (2) invariant/balance work — Al Hopfer's 2022
border↔interior multiset condition (msg 10754, the NS-1 source), balanced
rotation sets, capiman's invalid-combination mining, and the SAT thread that
literally never ends (it spans the entire window, 10707→11823); (3) complex
theory going executable and then getting its author back — McGavin's headline
numbers (14,702 / 4e-8, msg 11193) and complex_theory.c (msg 11197), followed by
Brendan Owen's return to the list in 2025 (msg 11500) with a refined join-
probability model (11546); and (4) the AI era arriving as farce before it
arrives as tool (ChatGPT "the solution has been found" msg 10993; the Base44
app saga of 2025–26). The window closes with the group's founder honoured in
absentia (Kronjuvel tribute, msg 11771) and the SAT thread mid-conversation.

### 2022-05-24 — ★ Hopfer states the border↔interior multiset condition (the NS-1 source)
- who: Al Hopfer; Richard
- what: Opening "While solving for a 14x14 - 196 solution.", Hopfer states that the outer edges of the 14x14 interior "must be in parity correctness with the internal images on all 56 border pieces": as he runs his 196-piece solver he tallies the 14x14's rim colours image-by-image (17 interior colours) so they never exceed what the border pieces' inward faces supply. Pressed by Richard he restates it exactly as a multiset equality: the 14x14's border "must have the same mixture (parity) of the internal images on all the 56 border pieces" (10757). He also flags its looseness himself — the parity may hold and a border solution still not exist (Question 2 in 10754). **This message, 10754, is the precise source of the "Hopfer 2022" NS-1/border-balance invariant our wiki cites generically** (web/src/lib/ns1-deficit.ts, RecordsView). Earlier roots: Owen 2007 (msgs 414/422/485, 2073/2098) and Hopfer's own 2009 balance doctrine (6842/6844/6960).
- source: msg_num 10754 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/10754 — "While solving for a 14x14 - 196 solution." (multiset restatement 10757)
- wiki-relevance: technique:border-balance, records

### 2024-01-13 — ★ Complex theory's headline numbers, then its C source (complex_theory.c)
- who: Peter McGavin; ironhide8391 (K.); Dieter von Holten
- what: Asked for solution-count estimates, McGavin states the canonical figures with sources: **14,702 expected solutions with just the start piece** ("probably accurate to within a factor of 2"), **0.00000004 with all 5 hints** — "very strongly suggesting… a single, unique solution" (11193), plus the group database comparing theory to empirical counts on the clue puzzles.
