## Notable events (chronological)

### 2007-11-01 — Brendan Owen describes "decreasing squares" search order and corner-frequency piece ordering
- who: Brendan Owen
- what: In the "All Quiet" thread Brendan explains he solved a 14x14 (E2-pieces sub-puzzle) using a decreasing-squares fill order after scanline stalled, ordering pieces by their frequency in enumerated corner squares. He states scanline is near-optimal for the full search but a different order wins for irregular boards. antminder later runs controlled tests of corner-frequency ordering on 12x12 sub-puzzles and finds the best configuration is restarts plus corner frequency as a bias, not a strict order.
- source: msg_num 3124 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/3124 — "All Quiet" (also 3121, 3136; antminder's tests 3167, 3271)
- wiki-relevance: technique:search-order, technique:corner-frequency

### 2007-11-02 — Brendan Owen posts empirical branching factors by depth; peak at 161 pieces placed
- who: Brendan Owen
- what: Using ~10 million random partials per depth, Brendan tabulates the branching factor at every depth of a hint-then-scanline search. The node count peaks at 161 pieces placed with about 10^46 partial boards, within one order of magnitude of his prior theory. This is the window's core quantitative picture of the search tree shape.
- source: msg_num 3147 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/3147 — "Branching factors"
- wiki-relevance: technique:branching-factor, history

### 2007-11-05 — Branching probability distribution measured; binomial model confirmed
- who: Brendan Owen; louis.verhaard
- what: Brendan runs four programs estimating the full probability distribution of branch counts at each search depth (keeping ~100M sampled nodes alive per depth). louis.verhaard suggests a simple binomial model should predict it; Brendan confirms the binomial fits the empirical data very well, "that is why I am so sure of the difficulty" of the puzzle.
- source: msg_num 3168 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/3168 — "Branching probability distribution" (binomial: 3240, confirmation 3241)

### 2007-11-11 — "Rotation Solutions": a suspected anomaly in the real piece set, investigated and closed
- who: Dave Clark (eternitynut); dmitri_ulitski; John Tromp; antminder
- what: Dave Clark finds his backtracker locates full-board "rotation sets" (an assignment of rotations to all 256 pieces making edge counts consistent) far more slowly on the real E2 pieces than on generated E2-like sets, hinting the real set might be special. John Tromp proposes the orientation/parity variant. dmitri_ulitski then solves rotation sets by integer linear programming (glpsol) in ~5 seconds each and measures no difference between real and simulated pieces; antminder's GA agrees, and the anomaly is written off as coincidence/algorithm-specific.
- source: msg_num 3245 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/3245 — "Rotation Solutions" (Tromp 3254; ILP result 3320, 3326; GA update 3443; closure 3447)
- wiki-relevance: technique:rotation-sets, technique:ilp

### 2007-11-12 — First full-tree size estimate from the binomial model: 2.4 x 10^52 nodes
- who: louis.verhaard; mjqxxxx (Michael Quist)
- what: louis.verhaard computes expected nodes-to-search per board cell for a 16x16 row-scan search using the binomial branching assumption. After mjqxxxx catches an arithmetic bug ((1/17)^2 = 1/289, not 1/389), the corrected bottom line is 2.367 x 10^52 nodes for the whole search tree, which Louis notes is still pessimistic versus empirical data.
- source: msg_num 3253 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/3253 — "Branching probability distribution" (initial calc 3250, correction 3251)
- wiki-relevance: technique:search-space-estimates

### 2007-11-19 — Piece numbering order explained; Selby/Riordan design role sourced
- who: markrmsheppard; Mike (mfield); Brendan Owen
- what: A suspected special "property of piece 256" is resolved: Mike explains the published piece numbering comes from a sort ("lowest side on tile faces down"), so piece 256 is just last in sort order — not a design signal. ### 2007-11-19 — Expected-solutions formula posted
- who: eternityiipuzzle
- what: Building on Brendan's numbers, posts the closed-form expected-solutions estimate: 4! x 56! x 195! x 4^195 x (1/5)^60 x (1/17)^420, with a discussion of the parity corrections that break the independence assumption for the last pieces placed. This is the same family of estimate later refined by kubzpa's paper.
- source: msg_num 3385 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/3385 — "Property of Piece 256"
- wiki-relevance: technique:solution-count

### 2008-01-29 — Dave Clark's Monckton phone call: how the puzzle was generated
- who: Dave Clark; Pelle Evensen
- what: Dave recounts a long phone conversation with Christopher Monckton (26 July 2007): the puzzle was generated from entropy typed by the judges (200 keyboard inputs), regenerated until the judges were happy, printed once and vaulted. Monckton described the PRNG as using "gaussian residues of powers of appropriately chosen primes", which Dave believes Selby/Riordan implemented themselves — raising the (theoretical) possibility of a cryptographically weak generator. Pelle Evensen suggests it may be Blum-Blum-Shub, which would be provably hard. Primary-source material on the puzzle's creation.
