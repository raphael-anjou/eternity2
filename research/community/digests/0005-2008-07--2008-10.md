### 2008-08-27 → 09-02 — Louis and Max compare high-score methods: both past the 463 "don't-talk-about-limit"
- who: louis.verhaard; Max (mm_4761)
- what: The key method exchange of the 467 era. Max says he combined backtracking heuristics with a mismatch-tolerant endgame and "was quite surprised that I was able to get beyond the 'don't-talk-about-limit' quite easily" (the limit being the publicly known 463). Louis replies "Max, you are really a dangerous man!", recounts his own path — hundreds of 248-piece/450 solutions, then discovering via Bob Cousins'/Dave Clark's solver (458 in a minute) that half-fitting placements score — and describes his method: DFS with forward pruning and hand-tuned static thresholds, heuristics chosen to maximize the tileability of the remaining pieces in the 160–200 piece region. Both measure heuristic strength by where the peak of the node-depth distribution sits: both just under 170 (equivalent to eliminating roughly one interior colour). Louis closes: "I look forward to interesting in-depth discussions about the techniques we use after new-year" — i.e. after the 31 Dec 2008 scrutiny date.

### 2008-10-05 — Max's border-piece scanline optimization: "finally something that beats simple scan-row"
- who: Max; louis.verhaard; antminder
- what: Discovered while placing pieces by hand in trans.spam's manual program: at a row start, border candidates that share the same interior colour (differing only in the unmatched second border colour) are equivalent — refuting one refutes them all.
