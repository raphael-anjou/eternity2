# Digest 0010 — 2010-09 → 2011-02
Coverage: 791 messages, msg_num 7965–8764.

Window context: the run-up to and passing of the FINAL scrutiny date
(2010-12-31). The contest ends with no winner; Tomy France's press release
(surfaced 2011-01-24) and the Tomy UK "Eternity II unclaimed" page
(2011-01-26) close the competition with no extension and — crucially — no
published solution, which splits the list between scam accusations and
defenders. Technically it is a strong window: Brendan Owen proves the
(1 − 1/e) max-node-depth result behind the "magic of 161", Peter McGavin
emerges as the resident complex-theory calculator, and after the deadline
the community pivots to Brendan's synthetic 10x10 as its shared benchmark.
Unverified score claims bloom in the vacuum (a "476 without the hint", a
462 partial photo, "almost 10 467s"); none is ever substantiated, and 467
remains the last verified milestone.

Archive note: msg_nums 8246, 8259, 8261 and 8633 are absent from the
groups.io export — 8246 almost certainly carried the original "476" claim
(see the 2011-01-08 event); it survives only through the replies.

### 2010-11-11 — The "magic of 161" proved: max node depth = 256·(1 − 1/e)
- who: juraj.pivovarov; brendan_owen
- what: Juraj asks where the oft-quoted depth 161 (where node counts peak in an optimal search order) comes from, then notices golden-ratio×256 ≈ 158 is suspiciously close. Brendan tries and fails to prove the golden-ratio version but, prompted by it, derives the true constant: the peak sits at fraction (1 − 1/e) of the board, and 256 × (1 − 1/e) = 161.8. A genuinely elegant closed-form result for the complexity model.
