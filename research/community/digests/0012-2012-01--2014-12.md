# Digest 0012 — 2012-01 → 2014-12
Coverage: 347 messages, msg_num 9005–9359.

Window context: the quiet years — three full years produce fewer messages than
a busy quarter of 2008 (83 in 2012, 130 in 2013, 134 in 2014). With the TOMY
contest dead, four things keep the list alive: (1) academia arriving in person
(Tony Wauters posts his group's hyper-heuristic journal paper and stays);
(2) a Czech retailer's 12,000 EUR re-run of the contest — the only money on
the table this era; (3) the May-2013 "Things running?" revival that turns
Brendan Owen's 10x10 benchmark into the communal battlefield, with Peter
McGavin as the window's authority figure (complex-theory estimates, a LaTeX
transcription of the theory, the 178/180 partial); and (4) a 2014 hardware/
speed renaissance — Michael Field's FPGA design and the arrival of Arnaud
Carré's 114M nodes/s solver. No new records on E2 itself: 467 stands the
entire window, and newcomer Dima's astonishment at that fact (msg 9318) is
the era in one line.

### 2013-05-13 — Few or many inner squares? McGavin's complex-theory numbers for the 14x14
- who: dvholten; Peter McGavin; Michel Gaillard; leenstrab
- what: dvholten conjectures E2 has "very very few" distinct inner-14x14 layouts, so a found 14x14 should frame easily (9108/9120). Peter McGavin answers with complex-theory-based estimates: ~6.0252e+30 14x14 interior solutions with the start piece placed, of which ~14,702 can be framed (odds 1 in 4.0982e+26 that a random one completes E2); with all four clues placed, ~1.6789e+19 interior solutions of which about ONE frames — the cleanest published statement of why hint pieces matter and why interior partials are worthless as progress. His own best is 358/364 interior edges. Michel Gaillard posts compatible edge-probability estimates (5e25 solutions in a 1e55 space, 9115).
- source: msg_num 9117 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/9117 — "Things running ?" (conjecture 9108/9120, Gaillard 9115, framed-14x14 challenge idea in same msg)
- wiki-relevance: technique:complex-theory, technique:hint-corners

### 2014-02-07 — Michael Field's FPGA solver: 5 billion placements/s per chip (design)
- who: Michael Field; capiman26061973; Bucas Jean-François; mulisak
- what: Field (who once held "one of the fastest" software backtrackers at 70M tiles/s per 1.5 GHz core via a C code generator) announces an FPGA design after a six-year itch: restricting backtracking to the top-left 15x15 with no hints makes every tile lookup identically indexed by top+left edges, shrinking the tables to ~4 KB and making the solver uniform enough for hardware — ~50 instances at 200 MHz on a Zynq 7020, up to 5 billion placements/s per chip projected. Progress reports follow (memory-bandwidth redesign 9232, simulator walking a 4x4 tree 9231/9236, fixed-corner symmetry bug found via Arnaud 9237). He also points a curious member to his own free FPGA ebook on GitHub (9245).
- source: msg_num 9226 — https://groups.io/g/eternity2/message/9226 — "Solving Eternity II in FPGA hardware" (tables 9228, redesign 9232, progress 9237, ebook 9245)
- wiki-relevance: technique:fpga, tooling, people
