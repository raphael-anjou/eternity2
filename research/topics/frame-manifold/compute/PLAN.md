# frame-manifold — reproduction plan

> **Repro tier: mixed: frame enumeration exact; revival depth probes seeded-statistical.**
> exact = re-run reproduces committed results byte-for-byte. seeded-statistical =
> committed results are the record of our run (hardware noted in the article);
> reproduction = fresh run agreeing within the stated tolerance. qualitative =
> the effect (sign/ordering/band) reappears; exact digits are lineage-bound.

## (a) The exact claim, with expected numbers

Source: the research vault, `vault/concepts/bb60-frame-manifold.md` and
`vault/concepts/fluid-frame-rim-swap.md` (vol-244, 2026-07-16). All numbers
below are cited from those pages.

Definitions. A **frame** is a placement of the 4 corner pieces and 56 edge
pieces on the 60 border cells of the official 16x16 board, grey exactly
outward. **BB** is the number of matched border-to-border adjacencies along
the ring, maximum 60; BB = 60 is a "perfect" frame. A **legal exchange**
swaps two placed frame pieces of the same class (corner with corner, edge
piece with edge piece); rotations are forced by the grey-outward rule, so an
exchange is determined by the slot pair. An exchange is **free** when
`bb_delta = 0`.

Claimed numbers to reproduce:

1. **Exchange cost distribution** on a BB = 60 frame (measured identical on
   three frames in the source): `bb_delta = -4`: ~931 (63.9%), `-3`: ~18
   (1.2%), `-2`: ~464 (31.8%), `0`: **45** (3.1%), `> 0`: **0**. The two
   structural claims are the last two rows: **no exchange ever gains BB**
   (necessarily, since 60 is the maximum) and **exactly 45 free exchanges**,
   the same count on every perfect frame tested.
2. **Composition without depletion**: after 1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100 chained
   free swaps (free set recomputed on the current ring each step), BB stays
   60/60 and 45 free moves remain available. After 100 chained swaps, 36 of
   60 ring slots differ from the start.
3. **Non-cosmetic**: 0 of the 45 free swaps preserve inward colour; after a
   random free walk, 39 of the 56 inward (rim-target) colours differ, and one
   walk visited 292 distinct rim-target vectors without leaving BB = 60.
4. **Dead frames**: over 500 BB = 60 frames, **180/500 (36.0%)** have at
   least one dead interior cell (a cell adjacent to the frame that zero of
   the 196 interior pieces can fill given the rim-imposed colours);
   **55/500 (11.0%)** are dead at the interior scan-start cell, which makes a
   scan-order DFS die at depth 0/196. Dead-cell histogram: 1 cell: 155,
   2 cells: 24, 3 cells: 1.
5. **Revival**: greedy repair using free swaps only revived **60/60 (100%)**
   of the first 60 dead frames to zero dead cells, BB never leaving 60. In
   the worked example, one free swap took a framed DFS from depth 0/196 to
   depth 153/196.

Out of scope (explicitly unresolved in the source): any effect on completed
board scores. The source's own A/B produced no completed framed boards, so
"revival raises the final score" is not a claim of this topic.

## (b) Scoring-convention mapping

The kit's `score_cells` counts every matched, non-grey right/down adjacency
on the 16x16 grid, maximum 480. This finding's quantities map onto subsets
of that count:

- **BB** (border-to-border, max 60) is the sub-count of the kit score over
  the 60 ring adjacencies (the right-adjacencies inside the top and bottom
  rows and the down-adjacencies inside the left and right columns). The
  compute bin derives BB by scoring the frame-only board with `score_cells`:
  with no interior pieces placed, the only scoreable adjacencies are the ring
  ones, so `score_cells(frame) = BB` exactly. No private scorer is needed.
- "Dead cell" and "depth" are not scores at all; they are feasibility facts
  about the piece set against the rim constraints, computed directly from
  piece edges.
- Strict-5/5 vs matched-edges does not arise: frames here are unhinted, as in
  the source measurement. Hint compatibility of frames is a separate concern
  and is not part of the claim.

## (c) Scale-faithfulness

This claim only makes sense on the **canonical 16x16 piece set**. The number
45 is claimed as a structural property of the real border pool: exactly 4
corners for 4 slots and 56 edge pieces for 56 slots (zero count slack, so
every move is a permutation of the ring), with 17 distinct inward colours
distributed 1 to 6 pieces per colour. A generated small-N instance has a
different border pool, a different inward-colour distribution, and a
different free-move count, so a small-N run can neither confirm nor refute
the 45. The dead-frame rate likewise depends on the real interior pool (196
pieces, 17 interior colours on the rim side). All measurements therefore run
on `official_instance(false)`.

The one deliberate deviation from the source: frames are generated by this
crate's own randomised DFS rather than taken from the original run's frame
files (which live in the research repo's untracked `output/`). The source
itself flags this as the desired robustness check ("worth checking on frames
from a different generator before treating 45 as universal"). Expected
consequences: claims 1 to 3 (the 45, no-gain, composition, non-cosmetic)
should replicate exactly; claim 4's 36.0% is a property of the original
generator's sampling and may shift, though the qualitative fact (a large
fraction of perfect frames have dead cells, most with exactly one) should
hold; claim 5's 100% revival should replicate.

## (d) Exact reproduction steps

```sh
cd research/topics/frame-manifold/compute
cargo run --release                        # default: 500 frames, walk length 100
cargo run --release -- --frames 50         # quick pass, ~seconds
cargo run --release -- --frames 500 --walk 100 --seed0 1 > ../results/manifold.json
```

The bin prints a JSON report with, per section: the pooled bb_delta
distribution and per-frame free counts (claim 1), the walk table at
checkpoints 1/5/10/25/50/100 with BB, free count, slots changed, inward
colours changed, and distinct rim vectors visited (claims 2 and 3), the
dead-frame census with the dead-cell histogram and the dead-at-scan-start
rate (claim 4), and the free-swap revival results over the dead frames
(claim 5).

Acceptance: free count = 45 on every frame; zero positive bb_delta over all
frames; BB = 60 and free count = 45 at every walk checkpoint; 0 free swaps
inward-colour-preserving; dead-frame share in the tens of percent with the
histogram dominated by single-cell deaths; revival rate 100% of dead frames
sampled.

Expected runtime: well under 5 minutes for 500 frames on one core (frame DFS
and exchange enumeration are both trivial; the exchange set has at most
C(56,2) + C(4,2) = 1546 members per frame). A preflight run of the built bin
at the default scale completed in about 7 seconds.

Note on distribution shape: the source reports ~1458 total exchanges per
frame (931 + 18 + 464 + 45). The slot-pair space is 1546; the gap suggests
the source excluded some no-op class (for example swaps of twin pieces or
same-inward-colour pairs). The bin reports raw slot-pair counts and, if the
total differs from 1458, the split of the difference, so the discrepancy is
surfaced rather than hidden. The load-bearing claims (45 free, 0 gains) are
unaffected.
